Cookie Policy

This document explains what you need to know about how Dell Software uses cookies on the Foglight web site, and how to manage or remove them if you wish to do so.

  1. Summary

    When you visit the Foglight web site, you can surf the web site anonymously and access important information without revealing your identity. In order to improve our web site, we use "cookies" to track your visit.

    On the Foglight web site users can manage and/or delete cookies as they wish. Information about how to do this is provided in the section How to manage cookies.

  2. About cookies

    Cookies are small data files which are placed by a web server on a user's computer browser and can be used for a number of activities, including facilitation of the site's functionality, and to assist with site usage analysis.

  3. How Foglight uses cookies

    While the Foglight web site uses cookies to track your visit to the web site, this information does not identify you personally and you remain anonymous unless you have otherwise provided the Foglight web site with personally identifiable information.

    A full list of all the cookies used across the Foglight web site can be seen in the List of cookies used on the Foglight web site.

    1. Analytics

      Foglight uses a range of analytics tools to collect information about how people use its sites. This helps us make sure the sites are meeting users' needs and to find out how we could improve.

      Analytics cookies store information about what pages people visit, how long they are on the site, how they got there and what they click on. Information supplied by cookies helps Foglight to analyze the profile of visitors and to provide them with a better experience.

      Analytics cookies do not collect or store users' personal information (for example, names or addresses), so this information cannot be used to identify individuals. Foglight does not allow analytics companies to use or share its analytics data.

    2. Profile management

      Some Foglight sites allow users to login and retrieve personal information and account details. Some of these sites use a cookie in order to remember account information.

  4. Third-party cookies

    Third-party cookies are not set directly by Foglight, but by third-party service or functionality providers.

    Foglight uses a number of suppliers who set cookies on the Foglight web site in order to deliver the services that they are providing (for example, web site analytics or advertising).

    In addition, the Foglight web site embeds 'share' buttons to enable users of the site to easily share articles with their friends through popular social networks, and also embeds Comm100 'Live chat' so that our support team can communicate effectively with customers. These sites may set a cookie when you are also logged in to their service.

    Foglight does not control the dissemination of these cookies. For more information about the cookies used by these suppliers, including how to opt-out, view their individual privacy policies listed below.

    Cookie Name Description of Cookie
    Google Analytics Google Analytics stores the information collected by the cookie on servers in the United States.   5. How to manage cookies

    Internet browsers allow users to manage their cookies. They can set their devices to accept all cookies, to let them know when a cookie has been set, or never to receive cookies.

    Each browser works in a different way: use the 'Help' menu in your browser to find out to change your cookie preferences. For information on how to do this on the browser of your mobile phone you should refer to your handset manual.

    Please be aware that disabling cookies completely means that certain personalized features will not then be provided and therefore you may not be able to make full use of all of the web site's features.

    Some cookies are not set by the Foglight web site but by other sites which provide a service or functionality on the Foglight web site. These are known as third-party cookies. Managing, deleting, or blocking these cookies requires you to visit the web site of these third parties. Details are provided in the section Third-party cookies.

    You can learn more about cookies and how to manage them at AboutCookies.org.
